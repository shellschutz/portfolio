HAPIGA's Mobile Games (2024 - 2025)

From October 2024 to August 2025, I took up a position as Game Designer for Hapiga Studio, a mobile game company based in Hanoi that mainly developes Casual and Puzzle titles. I entered first as an Intern for 2 months, afterwards I stayed as an official Game Designer.

Image: Company's holiday picture (That's me bottom right)

Projects Involved:

Car Maker: Trailblazer

Worked mainly on level designs as an intern. Helped create and play test many level, ensuring quality.

Video: Car Maker's Gameplay


Mr. Potato: Puzzle Master

Worked mainly as document keeper, level designers, and play tester. Started the project with other interns, promoted to official after the project's outstanding performance. Scoring the company's first chart position on Google Play's Casual ranking.

Video: Potato's Gameplay

Image: Potato's Document Main Page


Snake Out Puzzle

Worked mainly as document keeper, level designer, and play tester. One of the more difficult project with a variety of problems:

  • Competition has been ahead in level design and production.
  • Resources being pulled towards other company's project.
  • Management meddling: pressure to find the abstract difficulty formula, their screw-up in A/B testing that almost nullified the efforts spent on it, hesitation to add content, live-ops, or to compete further for that matter, etc.
  • My mistake at one point caused a soft lock and created a clear drop in player, which was a valuable lesson in play testing.

However, this project has helped me figure how to work with the developers better and it helps that I had the necessary technical skill to match them. It became a prelude to the next project's improvement in workflow.

Video: Snake Out's Gameplay


Drop Them Down: People Out

Worked mainly as document keeper, level designer, and play tester. For this project, I have agreed with our developer on a clear plan forward, we wanted to finish the prototype really early, 2 weeks is the target. During which I will figure out the components of the game, then create a JSON format that the developer will implement into the game, that upon parsing will become a complete level. That way, we will be co-developing the game without bottle necking each other's tasks.

Image: Drop Down Level's JSON format

Image: Level 8's design prototyped in Draw.io

Image: Level 8's JSON

It ended up working out really well and we met our personal deadline, already having over 100 levels by the end of those two weeks. The format admittedly however is pretty complicated, it was only meant to prototype quickly, after the game has proven its worth then a better editor tool is made.

Video: People Out's Gameplay


Reception/Reflection:

At the end of July 2025, I left Hapiga to pursue making my own indie game with pngTriads. I pretty much enjoyed most of my time at Hapiga, despite it having its ups and down, despite feeling that I was stretched thin sometimes, my colleagues had always helped me get through it. I appreciate the challenge and responsibility Hapiga placed on me during my time there and I hope the best for everyone there, especially my fellow designers who had always have my back.
